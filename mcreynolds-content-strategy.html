Executive Overview

The McReynolds Real Estate team specializes in serving military families PCSing to and from Northern California. This playbook outlines a data-driven content strategy designed to generate 45 booked calls per month and $20,000 in monthly revenue through strategic social media presence, educational content, and military-focused messaging.

Primary Goal

45 booked calls per month with qualified military families relocating to or from Travis AFB and Beale AFB regions.

Monthly Revenue Target

$20,000/month through lead generation and conversion from content-qualified prospects.

Content Capacity

C3 High — 2 hours of filming per month, weekly content sessions, robust repurposing strategy.

Budget Allocation

$1,000/month for content production, design, and potential paid promotion on social platforms.

Target Audience Profile

Who They Are

Military families (Active Duty, Retired, Veterans) relocating to Northern California for PCS assignments at Travis AFB, Beale AFB, or other installations in Solano, Napa, Yolo, and Sacramento counties.

Primary Pain Points

Budget & Affordability: Not knowing their BAH rate, unable to calculate home affordability, confused about VA loan benefits, worried about down payment requirements.

Objections & Barriers

"No money, no credit, no resources." First-time homebuyers, recent veterans with thin credit, families in financial transition, uncertain about home buying readiness.

Information Needs

Top FAQs: How soon can I sell? Rent or buy? Best neighborhoods? Best schools? Can I afford a house? What's the process? How does a VA loan work?

Platform-Specific Guides

Each platform operates differently algorithmically, culturally, and functionally. These guides optimize McReynolds' presence on each major platform with specific tactics, specs, and best practices.

📷 Instagram

Audience Profile:
929 followers (@travisafb_homefinder), primarily military families, spouses, military community
Primary Content:
Reels (60%), Feed posts (25%), Stories (daily)
Video Specs:
Reels: 9:16 vertical, 15-60 seconds, maximum 180MB
Feed: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (vertical), minimum 1080x1350px
Stories: 1080x1920px, maximum 15 seconds per story
Upload Schedule:
Reels: 4x per week (Mon, Wed, Thu, Fri)
Feed: 3x per week (Tue, Fri, Sat)
Stories: 2-3x daily throughout the week

Best Practices

  • Hook in first 1 second: Use text overlay, pattern interrupt, or bold movement. Algorithm counts early stops.
  • Maximize Reels: Instagram's algorithm heavily favors short-form video. 60% of content should be Reels.
  • Use captions on all videos: 80% of users watch without sound. Burned-in captions improve retention.
  • Call-to-action in every post: "Save this," "Share," "Comment your answer," "DM us," or "Click the link in bio."
  • Story stickers: Use polls, questions, sliders, and countdown stickers daily. High engagement = algorithm favor.
  • Hashtag strategy: 25-30 hashtags per post. Mix 5-10 high-volume (#realestate, #travisafb) with 15-20 niche (#travisafbhousing).
  • Optimal posting times: 7-8 AM, 12-1 PM, 6-7 PM. Military families check social during breaks.
  • Engagement within 1 hour: Respond to comments immediately (first hour is critical for algorithm). Reply to 30-50 comments per post.
  • Stories linking: Use link stickers in Stories to drive to website or lead capture pages (swipe-ups not available unless 10K+ followers, so use sticker strategy).
  • Collaborate with military influencers: Partner with base-adjacent creators for Reel duets, stitches, and cross-promotion.

▶️ YouTube

Channel Profile:
@pcscalifornia channel (existing), growth potential for "real estate + military + PCS" niche
Content Strategy:
Long-form education (primary), Shorts (secondary), playlist organization
Video Specs:
Long-form: 10-15 minutes, 1920x1080px (16:9), H.264 codec, MP4 format, under 256GB file size
Shorts: 9:16 vertical, 15-60 seconds, minimum 1080x1920px
Thumbnails: 1280x720px, under 2MB, high contrast, readable at small size
Upload Schedule:
Long-form: 1x per week (Wednesday, primary production day)
Shorts: 3-4x per week, pulled from long-form content

Best Practices

  • SEO Title (60 characters max): Front-load with keyword. Example: "VA Loan Guide for Travis AFB Military Families 2026"
  • Description (2000 character limit): First 100 characters visible before "more." Hook viewers. Include keywords naturally. Add timestamps, links, and chapter markers.
  • Keywords & Tags: Primary keyword in title, description first sentence. Add 10-15 tags, mix high and low competition. Use YouTube autocomplete for tag ideas.
  • Thumbnails matter: Create custom thumbnails for every video. Use contrasting colors (teal + white), text overlay with emotion, face if featured. Test A/B thumbnails.
  • First 10 seconds critical: Hook immediately with question, story setup, or bold statement. Average viewer decides within 10 seconds whether to continue.
  • Chapters & Timestamps: Add chapter markers (0:00 Intro, 1:23 First Topic, etc.). Improves user experience and time-on-page metrics.
  • Captions/Subtitles: Auto-captions work, but manually edit for accuracy (military terms, names, jargon). Improves accessibility and engagement.
  • Cards & End Screens: Add cards promoting other videos at 50%, 75%, 100% through video. Use end screen to promote playlist or channel subscription.
  • Playlist organization: Create playlists by content pillar (Mission Ready, VA Loans, Market Updates, Stories). Improves binge-watch and session duration.
  • Shorts strategy: Extract 30-60 second clips from long-form videos, add captions, hooks. Promote long-form video in description for click-through.
  • Optimal upload day/time: Tuesday-Thursday, 2-4 PM PST (when military families are active). YouTube rewards consistency and timeliness.
  • Community engagement: Respond to comments within 24 hours. Pin top comments. Post community posts (polls, behind-the-scenes) to keep audience active between videos.
