Content Fixes
6
Audit Last 30 Posts & Identify High-Performers

What to Change: You likely have a mix of content types. Some posts might have 50 likes, others 500. You need to identify the winners and replicate.

Why It Matters: If you don't know what performs, you'll keep posting low-engagement content. Instagram's algorithm punishes low-engagement posts by showing them to fewer people.

How to Do It:

  • Pull analytics from your last 30 posts (use Instagram Insights if you have a business account).
  • Track: likes, comments, shares, saves, profile visits, and follow rate per post.
  • Identify top 5 performers. What do they have in common? (Format, caption length, timing, content type?)
  • Identify bottom 5 underperformers. Delete or repurpose them.
  • Create a "winning template" document: post type, caption style, hashtag count, timing, etc.
Expected Impact
30-40% improvement in average post engagement. This creates a feedback loop: better engagement = algorithm shows to more people = more followers = more leads.
7
Add Hooks to Every Video (First 1-3 Seconds)

What to Change: Videos likely start without a hook. First 3 seconds are critical—most people scroll past slow intros.

Why It Matters: Instagram/TikTok algorithm measures "watch time." If 70% of viewers skip in the first 3 seconds, the algorithm throttles your post. A good hook = viewers stay, algorithm promotes more.

How to Do It:

  • Every video needs a military-specific hook: "If you're PCSing to Travis AFB, watch this..." or "This VA loan myth is costing you $50K..."
  • Use text overlay in first 2 seconds. Make it bold, contrasting, benefit-driven.
  • Examples: "3 neighborhoods military families love," "The ONE question before buying," "What no one tells you about BAH."
  • Use trending audio (military-relevant or upbeat) to increase algorithmic push.
Expected Impact
50-70% increase in video completion rate. Higher completion rate signals quality to algorithm, increasing reach to 2-3x the audience.
8
Create 3 Signature Content Series

What to Change: Your content is likely random posts without a recurring structure. Audiences love series because they know what to expect.

Why It Matters: Series create anticipation and habitual viewing. Military families will follow specifically for "PCS 101" videos if you post them weekly. Series also make your job easier—less creative burden.

How to Do It:

  • Series 1: "PCS 101" — Weekly educational reels (Mondays). Topics: preparing for PCS, timeline breakdown, costs, VA loan eligibility, etc. 60-90 seconds.
  • Series 2: "Neighborhood Spotlight" — Weekly neighborhood tours (Saturdays). Walk through a Vacaville/Fairfield/Dixon neighborhood, highlight schools, parks, military community. 2-3 minutes.
  • Series 3: "VA Loan Myths" — Bi-weekly myth-busting (Thursdays). Tackle common misconceptions: "VA loans have higher interest," "You need a down payment," etc. 45-60 seconds.
Expected Impact
Followers will anticipate and seek your content. Expected 50% increase in video shares and saves, leading to 25-30% follower growth within 60 days.
9
Rewrite Every Caption (Hook + Value + CTA)

What to Change: Captions likely start with descriptions ("Beautiful home in Vacaville..."). This doesn't hook readers or drive action.

Why It Matters: Captions drive engagement and CTAs. A weak caption = fewer comments, fewer saves, fewer DMs, fewer leads.

How to Do It:

  • Line 1 (Hook): Start with benefit or question. "PCSing to Travis AFB? Here's what you need to know..." or "This neighborhood just became more affordable..."
  • Lines 2-5 (Value): Deliver the promised value. 3-4 key points or insights.
  • Final Line (CTA): Always end with a clear ask. "Save this for your PCS planning," "DM 'CONSULT' to book your call," "Drop a comment with your favorite neighborhood."
  • Keep total caption 100-150 words (readability).
Expected Impact
25-35% increase in comments and DM inquiries. Better captions = more engagement signals = algorithm promotes more = exponential reach growth.
10
Add CTAs to EVERY Post (Explicit Asks)

What to Change: Posts likely lack explicit calls-to-action. Passive posts get passive results.

Why It Matters: A 1,000-person impression with no CTA = 0 conversions. A 500-person impression with a strong CTA = 10-20 conversions. CTAs drive action.

How to Do It:

  • Educational posts: "Save this for later" or "Share with someone PCSing soon."
  • Neighborhood tours: "Is this your future home? Comment below or DM 'TOUR' for a viewing."
  • Testimonial/proof posts: "Want a similar story? Book your free consultation — link in bio."
  • Service-focused posts: "Questions about VA loans? DM 'VA' and we'll walk you through it."
  • Rotate CTAs so they feel fresh, not spammy.
Expected Impact
Direct correlation: explicit CTAs drive 3-5x more conversions than passive posts. Expected 50+ additional booking requests per month once implemented consistently.
Engagement Tactics
11
Respond to Every Comment Within 2 Hours

What to Change: Comments likely go unanswered or take days to respond. This signals you're not engaged with your community.

Why It Matters: Instagram's algorithm prioritizes "conversation" over passive likes. Comments are gold. A post with 20 comments outranks a post with 200 likes. Fast response times = more comments = algorithm boost.

How to Do It:

  • Set a phone alarm for 2 hours after posting to check comments.
  • Respond to every comment (no exceptions). Even one-word questions deserve thorough answers.
  • Use responses to start conversations: "Great question! This is what many military families ask. Here's the answer..." + emoji.
  • Respond to replies to your replies (go two levels deep).
  • Consider hiring a VA to monitor comments outside your active hours.
Expected Impact
Comments are conversation starters that lead to DMs. Expect 20-30% of commenters to slide into DMs within 24 hours. On 10+ posts/week, this is 20-30 new DM conversations weekly.
12
Engage with Travis AFB Community Pages & Military Groups

What to Change: You're likely not actively engaging in community spaces where your audience congregates. You need to go where military families are.

Why It Matters: Community engagement builds warm relationships and referrals. Military spouses trust other military spouses. If you're active in Travis AFB Facebook groups and engaged with their posts, you become a familiar, trusted resource.

How to Do It:

  • Find 10-15 Travis AFB and military family Facebook groups (search "Travis AFB moms," "PCS to California," "Military families California").
  • Join each group (don't immediately promote).
  • Spend 1-2 weeks commenting on posts, answering questions, being helpful (no self-promotion).
  • Once you're established, selectively share relevant content ("If anyone's PCS-ing, I put together this guide...").
  • Post once or twice per week max in these groups (don't spam).
Expected Impact
Warm relationships = higher conversion rate. Expect 5-10 referrals/month from group members who know and trust you. These convert at 30-40% vs cold leads at 5-10%.
13
Create "PCS Checklist" Lead Magnet (Free PDF)

What to Change: You likely don't have a clear lead magnet. Military families should have an easy way to give you their email in exchange for value.

Why It Matters: Email is higher-intent than social followers. A "PCS Checklist" lead magnet captures families actively planning a move. They'll be warm leads, not cold prospects.

How to Do It:

  • Create a 2-3 page PDF: "The Complete PCS Checklist for Military Families Moving to Northern California."
  • Include: timeline, preparation steps, what to expect in each county, schools/neighborhoods overview, VA loan basics, closing timeline, moving costs estimate, emergency contacts.
  • Use Canva (free) or hire a designer (Fiverr $50-100) to make it look professional.
  • Use a landing page tool (LeadPages, Unbounce, or even Google Forms) to collect email in exchange for PDF.
  • Promote PDF: "DM 'CHECKLIST' for the free PCS guide" in every caption, story, and pinned comment.
Expected Impact
High-intent email list. Expected 50-100 email signups in first 60 days. These convert to calls at 15-20% (much higher than cold followers).
14
Do Weekly Q&A Stories/Posts (FAQ-Driven Content)

What to Change: You're not systematically addressing the FAQs military families ask. You have the answers; make them visible.

Why It Matters: FAQ-driven content performs exceptionally well because it answers real questions people are searching. It's high-intent content. Plus, it positions you as the go-to expert.

How to Do It:

  • Identify your top 5-10 FAQs: "How soon can I sell?", "Rent or sell?", "Best neighborhood?", "Best schools?", "Can I afford a house?", "VA loan myths?", "PCS timeline?", "BAH in California?"
  • Every Thursday: post one FAQ answer as a carousel, story series, or reel.
  • Use format: "FAQ: [Question]" + clear answer with visuals/graphics.
  • Encourage followers: "What's your PCS question? Drop it below and we'll feature it next week."
Expected Impact
High engagement (searches naturally lead to this content). Expected 30-50% higher save/share rate than typical posts. Builds authority and trust.
15
Collaborate with Local Businesses & Military Services

What to Change: You're operating as a solo presence. Strategic partnerships expand your reach to warm audiences.

Why It Matters: A collaborative post with a popular military spouse account, VA loan specialist, or local business reaches their entire audience. Cross-promotion multiplies reach and adds credibility.

How to Do It:

  • Identify 10-15 potential partners: military spouse influencers, VA loan specialists, moving companies, real estate photographers, property managers, military-adjacent services.
  • Reach out with a collaboration pitch: "We'd love to co-create content for military families. We have X followers interested in PCS. We'd feature your expertise to them. You do the same for us."
  • Collaborative content ideas: joint live Q&A, shared reels, carousel posts, guest takeovers.
  • Schedule 2-3 collaborations per month. Aim for partners with 500-5,000 followers (not mega-influencers, just solid accounts with engaged audiences).
Expected Impact
Each collaboration reaches 500-2,000 new warm prospects. 3 collaborations/month = 1,500-6,000 new impressions from high-intent audiences. Expected 10-20 new followers + 5-10 DM inquiries per collaboration.

Posting Schedule Best Practices:

  • Optimal Timing: Post Monday-Friday between 10am-2pm or 6pm-9pm (military families check social during breaks or evenings). Experiment and track engagement times.
  • Consistency > Perfection: Post on schedule even if content isn't flawless. Algorithm rewards consistency. A mediocre post on time beats a perfect post late.
  • Batch Content Creation: Don't film daily. Pick one day (Sunday) to batch-create 4-5 videos/reels for the week. Saves time and ensures quality.
  • Use a Content Calendar: Plan posts 2 weeks in advance. Use a tool like Later, Buffer, or Google Sheet. This prevents last-minute scrambling.
  • Repurpose Content: A Monday reel works as a TikTok, YouTube Short, and Instagram Reels. One piece = multiple formats.
  • Engagement Windows: The 2 hours after posting are critical. Be available to respond to comments and DMs immediately.

30-60 sec clips of satisfied clients talking about their experience. Proof >> any claims you can make. Include diversity of client types (buyers, sellers, relocating families).
Ideal Content: "We sold in 2 weeks," "VA loan process was seamless," "Sarah helped us find our dream home," family testimonials.
Video testimonials convert at 2-3x higher rate than written reviews or claims. Military families trust peer reviews more than marketing.
Interactive Stories & Polls
Use Instagram Stories' poll, question, and quiz features. Ask "Best neighborhood for families?" or "Rent or buy?" Keep audiences engaged weekly.
Ideal Content: Polls on neighborhoods, buyer vs. seller questions, military family questions, trending topics.
Interactive stories = direct audience feedback + engagement signals + relationship building. Stories don't count toward algorithm as much, but they deepen relationships.
Educational Carousel Graphics
Text-heavy slides breaking down complex info into digestible chunks. "7 Steps to Getting a VA Loan," "PCS Cost Breakdown," "Mortgage Pre-Approval Process."
Ideal Content: Infographics, checklists, timelines, breakdowns, how-tos.
Educational content is evergreen (doesn't expire). High save rate = signals quality to algorithm. Becomes searchable resource people reference for months.
