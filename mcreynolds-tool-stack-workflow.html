    Why This Math Matters

    McReynolds' goal is 45 booked calls per month. Each booked call represents a military family potentially buying or selling a home (average commission: $5,000-10,000). To get 45 booked calls, they need visibility. This visibility comes from consistent, frequent posting across all platforms where military families spend time.

    The Engagement Compounding Effect

    More posts = more visibility = more engagement = more leads. But it's not linear. Here's why:

    • Algorithm Boost: Instagram and TikTok algorithms favor consistent posting. 1 post/week gets minimal reach. 5-6 posts/week gets exponential reach. Each post you publish increases visibility of previous posts.
    • Multiple Touchpoints: A military family deciding to PCS to Travis AFB might see one McReynolds TikTok, then a YouTube Shorts the next day, then an Instagram Reel, then a Google Review. By the 3-4th touchpoint, they click the GHL booking link. One lead requires multiple exposures.
    • Evergreen Content: These posts don't expire. A YouTube video about "5 Military Family Questions We Always Get Asked" posted 6 months ago still shows up in search results and generates leads today.
    • Platform Diversity: Military families use different platforms. Older spouses might prefer Facebook. Younger officers use TikTok. Enlisted personnel follow Instagram. By being on all 4 platforms daily, McReynolds captures the full demographic.

    Content Breakdown by Type

    Here's what each piece of content is designed to accomplish in the McReynolds funnel:

    Long-Form YouTube Videos (2-3/month)

    Purpose: Build authority and capture search traffic. Military families searching "best neighborhoods for military families near Travis AFB" or "how to buy a home as military in California" should find McReynolds videos in search results.

    Topics: Home buying 101, PCS relocation guide, military housing benefits, top neighborhoods, VA loan guide, investment property tips, etc.

    Lead Value: High. People watching long-form videos are seriously researching. The GHL booking link in the description captures interested prospects.

    YouTube Shorts (12-16/month)

    Purpose: Reach new audiences. Shorts have viral potential and appear on the YouTube home feed for non-subscribers. A 45-second short about "3 Things Military Families Should Know About Travis AFB" can get 2,000-5,000 views.

    Topics: Quick tips, surprising facts, client testimonials, market updates, behind-the-scenes, trending sounds with military twist, etc.

    Lead Value: Medium. Viewers are entertained but may not be ready to book. However, they follow the channel, and future videos can convert them.

    Instagram Reels (12-16/month)

    Purpose: Build brand recognition and Instagram followers. Reels are Instagram's fastest-growing format. High-engagement Reels get placed on the Explore page and reach non-followers.

    Topics: Quick home tours, lifestyle content, client stories, before/after home renovations, funny military-related skits, etc.

    Lead Value: Medium-High. Instagram followers are warm prospects. They've seen your content multiple times, trust the brand. High propensity to click the bio link and book.

    TikToks (12-16/month)

    Purpose: Reach the youngest demographic (Gen Z military spouses, officers' adult children). TikTok has massive reach potential and lowest algorithmic barrier to entry. A single well-performing TikTok can get 50,000-200,000 views.

    Topics: Trending sounds with real estate twist, funny home shopping fails, military relocation reality, "day in the life" content, etc.

    Lead Value: Lower immediate, but huge awareness value. Many TikTok viewers eventually move to other platforms and remember the McReynolds brand when they're ready to buy.

    Instagram Stories (10+/month)

    Purpose: Stay top-of-mind with existing followers. Stories have a 24-hour lifecycle but drive massive engagement from your most engaged followers.

    Topics: Day-in-the-life, quick market updates, new listings, team celebrations, client wins, etc.

    Lead Value: Medium. Stories viewers are already followers. The goal is to keep them engaged until they're ready to book.

    Carousels (6-8/month)

    Purpose: Deep dives on topics people want to learn about. Carousels perform better than single-image posts because people swipe through all 5-10 slides.

    Topics: "5-Step Home Buying Checklist," "Top 10 Neighborhoods at Travis AFB," "Military Relocation Timeline," etc.

    Lead Value: High. Swiping through all slides shows high interest. The CTA on the last slide can drive to GHL booking page.

    Facebook Posts (6-8/month)

    Purpose: Reach older demographics and use Facebook's superior local targeting. Facebook Ads can micro-target "active military within 50 miles of Travis AFB" or "people interested in real estate who live in California."

    Topics: Longer-form posts with home tours, detailed market analysis, community spotlights, etc.

    Lead Value: Medium. Facebook audience skews older but often has more buying power. Combined with Facebook Ads, ROI can be high.

    Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

    These are step-by-step processes for recurring tasks. Each SOP can be executed by Sarah, Ben, or a delegated team member. The goal is consistency, quality, and speed. When in doubt, refer to the relevant SOP.

    SOP 1: YouTube Long-Form Video Production
    1 Choose topic from Notion content calendar.
    Check what content worked last month in analytics. Pick a topic that aligns with McReynolds' pillars: Local Market Insights, Military/PCS Guides, Home Tours, Client Success Stories, or Lifestyle.
    2 Research competitor videos (3-5 videos on same topic).
    Search YouTube for your topic. Use TubeBuddy to see which videos rank. Watch them. Note: strong intros, video structure, key points they cover. Don't copy — use this to inform your angle and ensure you're not missing important info.
    3 Write hook + outline (5-10 minutes).
    Hook: First 15 seconds must grab attention. Example: "I've sold 200+ homes to military families moving to Travis AFB. Here's what they always ask us." Outline: 3-5 main points you'll cover. Example: Point 1 = Home prices, Point 2 = Best neighborhoods, Point 3 = Military benefits, Point 4 = Timeline.
    4 Film in studio or on-location (15-20 minutes per video).
    Natural talking head format. Casual and conversational, not scripted. Sit in front of branded background (teal/black). Talk directly to camera. Film 2-3 takes of same content to have options in editing. Smile. Be confident. Remember your audience: military families who are nervous about moving.
    5 Edit in CapCut or DaVinci Resolve (30-45 minutes).
    Import your best take. Add intro/outro clips if available. Auto-caption using CapCut's feature (English language, readable font). Color correct if needed. Add background music (royalty-free, not too loud). Add text overlays for key points. Export in 1080p MP4 format.
    6 Create thumbnail in Canva (2-3 minutes).
    Use your YouTube thumbnail template from Canva. Upload a face photo or arrow graphic. Bold headline text (2-3 words max). Teal and black with white text. Download as PNG. Upload to Google Drive "Thumbnails" folder with video name.
    7 Write SEO title, description, and tags (10 minutes).
    Title: Include main keyword. Example: "PCS to Travis AFB? 5 Things Military Families Should Know About Buying Here" (65 characters). Description: First sentence = hook repeated. Next 5-6 sentences = key points from video. Include GHL booking link. Tags: 10-15 tags from TubeBuddy research (focus on military/PCS keywords).
    8 Upload and schedule in YouTube Studio.
    YouTube Studio → Create Video. Upload edited MP4. Upload custom thumbnail. Paste title, description, tags. Privacy: Public. Scheduled release date: Wednesday 9 AM (high engagement time for military audience). Add to playlist "Travis AFB Real Estate" so it's easy for viewers to find related content.
    9 Extract YouTube Shorts from the long-form video.
    Use CapCut. Find the 2-3 best 30-60 second sections of the video. Cut those sections. Add captions, music, trending sounds. These become standalone YouTube Shorts. Upload to YouTube as Shorts (separate from the long-form video).
    10 Cross-post Shorts to Instagram Reels and TikTok.
    Export the YouTube Shorts video file. In CapCut, adjust dimensions for Instagram (square 1080x1080 or vertical 1080x1350). Add Instagram-specific captions. Schedule in Later for Instagram Reels. Export again for TikTok (vertical, 9:16 aspect ratio). Schedule in Later for TikTok. Use same caption with platform-specific hashtags.
    SOP 2: Instagram Reel Production
    1 Select raw footage (30-90 second clips from batch filming).
    Review all short-form clips from the monthly filming session. Choose 4-5 that have strong visual appeal or surprising moments. Examples: quick home tour walkthrough, client testimonial reaction, market update, before/after transformation.
    2 Edit in CapCut for Instagram (15-20 minutes per Reel).
    Import clip. Trim to 15-90 seconds (optimal length for engagement). Auto-caption. Add trending audio (CapCut's audio library or TikTok trending sounds). Add text overlays (brand colors: teal text on black background). Add transitions if needed. Export as 1080x1350 (portrait) MP4.
    3 Write engaging caption and hashtags.
    Caption should be 2-3 sentences. Ask a question if possible (boosts comments). Example: "Which neighborhood would YOU recommend to a military family new to Travis AFB? Comment below!" Hashtags: 15-20 mix of broad (#realestate, #militaryfamily) and specific (#TravisAFB, #PCSmilitary). Use hashtag template from Notion.
    4 Schedule in Later/Metricool for optimal posting time.
    Later recommends best times based on your audience. For McReynolds, likely 12 PM (lunch break) or 6 PM (after work). Schedule 4-5 Reels across the next 1-2 weeks so they don't compete with each other. Space them 2-3 days apart for maximum reach.
    5 Monitor first 2 hours for engagement.
    When the Reel posts, check it immediately. Like comments. Reply to first 5-10 comments with thoughtful responses. This signals to Instagram's algorithm that the post is engaging and boosts visibility. Responds build community and can qualify leads (if someone asks about homes, reply with GHL link).
    SOP 3: Monthly Content Batch Day Checklist
    1 PRE-SHOOT (Day before filming)
    Confirm with Sarah & Ben that filming is happening. Review Notion content calendar. Create shot list: 2-3 YouTube video topics, 5-6 short-form clip ideas. Prep any props (home decor, staging). Check camera battery, lighting, ring light. Book any location or client for featured content. Confirm wardrobe (solid colors, teal if possible).
    2 DURING SHOOT (2 hours — batched into 1 focused session)
    Hour 1: Film 2-3 YouTube long-form videos (10-15 min talking head format per video). Use shot list. Multiple takes of same content. Smile and be conversational. Hour 2: Film 5-6 short-form clips (30-90 sec each). Includes: quick market updates, testimonials, home features, lifestyle, tips. Raw footage doesn't need to be perfect — editing will clean it up.
    3 POST-SHOOT IMPORT & ORGANIZATION
    Download camera footage immediately. Create Google Drive folder: "Content 2025/February 2025/Raw Footage." Upload all videos. Create subfolders: "YouTube Long-Form" and "Short-Form Clips." Rename files with format: "YYYY-MM-Topic-Take1.mp4" so you can find best takes easily. Share folder with editing person if delegated.
    SOP 4: GHL Lead Follow-Up Sequence
    1 Lead clicks GHL booking link from social (immediate trigger).
    Prospect sees McReynolds Instagram Reel about military home buying. Clicks GHL booking link. Lands on "Free PCS Consultation" booking page. Enters name, email, phone. Selects 15-min time slot with Sarah or Ben.
    2 Automatic thank-you email (immediately after booking).
    GHL auto-sends email: "Thanks for booking! Here's your confirmation details. Here's 5 resources about PCS to Travis AFB. Looking forward to talking with you!" Includes: appointment time, Zoom link, contact info, resource PDF. Sets tone: professional, helpful, eager.
    3 Automated SMS reminder (30-60 min before call).
    GHL auto-sends SMS: "Hi [Name], reminder: your PCS consultation with Sarah McReynolds is in 30 minutes. Click here for Zoom link: [link]. Any questions, reply to this text." Reduces no-shows.
    4 15-minute consultation call (Sarah or Ben).
    Listen more than you talk. Ask: What's your PCS timeline? Are you buying or selling? VA loan eligible? Biggest concern about moving to Travis AFB? Take notes in GHL contact record. Don't hard-sell. Goal: qualify if they're serious + build relationship.
    5 Post-call follow-up email (same day, within 1 hour).
    Personalized email: "Thanks for the chat! Based on what you shared, here are 3 neighborhoods I think would work for your family. Here's a link to our buyer's guide. Next step: I'll send you a list of homes that just hit the market in those areas. Talk soon!"
    6 Nurture sequence (triggered if they don't reply within 3 days).
    Day 3: Follow-up text "Hey [Name], sending over that neighborhood guide. Any questions?" Day 7: Email "Just checking in. Found 2 new homes that match what you're looking for. Thoughts?" Day 14: Email with market report "Military families who waited 30 days to buy wished they hadn't. Here's what inventory looks like now." Keep in touch without being pushy.
    7 Track in GHL pipeline for analytics.
    Update prospect's status in GHL: "Consultation Complete" → "Hot Prospect" → "Viewing Homes" → "Made Offer" → "Closed." Tag with lead source (e.g., "Instagram Reel Jan 15"). This tells you which social posts actually generate clients. Over time, you'll see patterns: "TikToks convert 10%, Instagram Reels convert 25%, YouTube videos convert 15%." Invest more in what works.
    SOP 5: Weekly Analytics Review
    1 Check Later/Metricool analytics (Monday 9 AM).
    View: Last week's posts. Which had most likes, comments, shares, saves? Which had highest engagement rate? Later provides this summary. Note: saves are more valuable than likes (saves = person wants to reference this later, showing high intent).
    2 Check platform native analytics (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok).
    YouTube Studio: Which videos got most watch time? What's your average viewer retention (goal: 50%+)? Instagram Insights: Which Reels hit Explore page? TikTok Analytics: Which videos have the highest view count? TikTok videos can go viral even with smaller follower counts. Track which videos got shared/saved most (highest intent metric).
    3 Check GHL for lead source data.
    GHL → Analytics → Lead Source. Look at which social posts generated booked calls last week. Example: "Instagram Reel posted Jan 15 ('5 Things Military Families Ask'): 3 booked calls. YouTube video posted Jan 10 ('PCS Timeline Guide'): 5 booked calls." This data is GOLD. It tells you exactly what content resonates with your actual audience.
    4 Note patterns and update Notion strategy.
    Observation: "Long-form YouTube videos consistently generate 20-30% more GHL clicks than TikToks." Action: Next month, film 3 YouTube long-forms instead of 2. Observation: "Client testimonial videos get 2x engagement." Action: Schedule monthly client testimonial filming. Observation: "Posts with 3+ hashtags underperform." Action: Use hashtag template from Notion (which has 15-20 optimized hashtags per topic).
    5 Plan next month's filming topics based on what worked.
    Look at your highest-performing content themes. If "Military Home Buying Guides" got 40% of engagement, plan 2-3 videos on that theme next month. If "Neighborhood Tours" consistently convert to leads, schedule monthly neighborhood filming. This keeps content aligned with audience interests AND lead generation goals.
