How social media converts into business. The path from awareness to closed deal, with specific metrics and benchmarks tied to your $28M annual sales and 45 booked calls target.

2. Engagement & Value Consumption

Viewer engages with content (likes, comments, shares, watch time). Educational content builds trust. Military families realize you understand their journey. Engagement rate target: 3%+ (from current 1-2%). Comments with military background questions indicate high-intent.

3. Interest Qualification

Strategic CTAs on content: "Are you PCSing? Save this post." "Comment 'READY' if interested in a free consultation." "DM us about your PCS timeline." Bio link to lead magnet (PCS Checklist, VA Loan Guide PDF). Target: 500-1K qualified leads/month in Phase 2-3.

4. Lead Capture & Nurture

Leads land on website, download lead magnet, enter email. Email sequence begins: Welcome email, 3-5 value-driven follow-ups (tips, guides, testimonials), soft CTA to book consultation. Email open rate target: 30-40% (military audience, relevant content). Click-through to booking: 5-10%.

↓

5. Consultation & Sales Conversation

Lead books 30-min consultation call. This is where your military expertise closes. Conversion rate: Military families who book a call with a veteran agent have 60-70% show rate and 40-50% "ready to move forward" rate. You're targeting 45 booked calls/month (Phase 3).

↓

6. Listing or Buying Agreement

Consultation converts to engagement (listing or buyer agreement). Average deal size: ~$500K. At 56 deals/year ($28M), you're closing ~5 deals/month from all sources. Social media should drive 40-60% of these (2-3 deals/month by year-end = 8-10 booked calls converting to transactions).

↓

7. Transaction Close & Referral

Closed transaction at ~$500K average. Commission at typical 2.5% per side = $12.5K revenue per deal. 10 deals/month from social pipeline = $125K/month revenue (goal: $20K/month from lead generation = conservative 3 deals/month = $37.5K revenue). Satisfied clients generate referrals to other military families.

Attribution & Tracking

How to Know What's Working: Use UTM parameters on all links: utm_source=instagram, utm_medium=bio_link, utm_campaign=pcs_checklist. This tracks which platform/post drives actual leads and conversions in Google Analytics.

CRM Integration: When leads book calls, tag them with source (e.g., "instagram_reel_pcs_tips"). Over time, you'll see patterns: Instagram Reels drive 40% of calls, YouTube drives 35%, TikTok 10%, direct/referral 15%. This informs where to double down effort and ad spend.

Monthly Reporting: Track impressions, engagement, clicks, lead captures, call bookings, and closed deals by source. Share with team weekly to celebrate wins and adjust strategy as needed.

Goal: By Month 12, social media should account for 50%+ of booked calls (vs. current referrals and local ads). This demonstrates the channel's value and justifies ongoing investment and team dedication.

Monthly Reporting Template

Every month, review:

  • Platform Growth: Follower growth vs. target. Which platforms gained fastest?
  • Engagement Quality: Which content types (carousels, Reels, videos, text posts) drive highest engagement and comments?
  • Lead Generation: How many emails captured? Cost per lead from social?
  • Conversion: Of booked calls, how many came directly from social source?
  • Content Performance: Which pillar topics performed best? Any surprising engagement wins?
  • Bottlenecks: What's making it hard to hit targets? Scheduling, content ideas, technical issues?
  • Next Month Adjustments: Double down on top-performing content. Sunset underperforming formats.

Tools to Use: Spreadsheet or dashboard tool (Google Sheets, Airtable, or Meta Business Suite's native analytics). Screenshots from each platform's analytics saved monthly. CRM reports filtered by "social" source. This takes 1-2 hours/month and creates accountability.

Team Accountability: Share monthly report with Sarah and Ben. Celebrate wins. Discuss challenges. This keeps the strategy top-of-mind and ensures consistency even if one person is managing day-to-day execution.

Red Flags & Course Corrections

If Instagram engagement stays under 2% after Month 2: Audit captions (CTAs may be weak). Audit posting times. Review feed aesthetic. Are posts aligned to 5 pillars or scattered?

If YouTube subs aren't growing after 8 weeks of consistent uploads: Check SEO — are titles/descriptions optimized for search? Are videos showing up in search results for your target keywords? Review watch time and audience retention. If people drop off at 30 seconds, the hook is weak.

If lead capture rate is below 5% despite high engagement: Lead magnet isn't compelling enough, OR the CTA isn't clear. Test new lead magnets. Add social proof (testimonials) to landing page.

If Phase 1 targets aren't hit by Month 4: Don't panic. Social growth is cumulative. Review what IS working and lean harder into it. Consider that organic growth may be slower than expected, requiring earlier shift to paid amplification (Phase 3 accelerator).
