Red Flags & Course Corrections

If Instagram engagement stays under 2% after Month 2: Audit captions (CTAs may be weak). Audit posting times. Review feed aesthetic. Are posts aligned to 5 pillars or scattered?

If YouTube subs aren't growing after 8 weeks of consistent uploads: Check SEO — are titles/descriptions optimized for search? Are videos showing up in search results for your target keywords? Review watch time and audience retention. If people drop off at 30 seconds, the hook is weak.

If lead capture rate is below 5% despite high engagement: Lead magnet isn't compelling enough, OR the CTA isn't clear. Test new lead magnets. Add social proof (testimonials) to landing page.

If Phase 1 targets aren't hit by Month 4: Don't panic. Social growth is cumulative. Review what IS working and lean harder into it. Consider that organic growth may be slower than expected, requiring earlier shift to paid amplification (Phase 3 accelerator).

    Balance production value with consistency. Higher tier doesn't always mean better results. Tier 3 raw content often outperforms Tier 1 in engagement because it feels authentic.

    Tier 1 — 1-2x/week
    Cinematic / Signature Content
    Highest production value. Goal: Authority building + SEO discovery
    Filming: Professional drone B-roll, neighborhood cinematography, studio lighting for talking heads, on-location walkthroughs
    Editing: Smooth transitions, color grading, licensed music, motion graphics, captions
    Length: 8-15 minutes (YouTube long-form)
    Platforms: YouTube (primary), cross-posted as Reels/Shorts (30-90 sec edit)
    Examples: Neighborhood tour with 4K drone footage, VA loan comprehensive guide, "How we help deployed buyers" deep dive
    Production Day: 8 hours filming + 6 hours editing per video
    Tier 2 — 2-3x/week
    Story-Wrapped Education
    Medium production. Goal: Engagement + saves + shares
    Filming: Talking head with B-roll inserts, screen recordings for market data, on-location walkthroughs, client testimonials
    Editing: Quick cuts, captions, basic color correction, stock music
    Length: 2-5 minutes
    Platforms: Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Facebook
    Examples: "3 neighborhoods under $500K near Travis", "VA loan Q&A", "Fairfield vs Suisun comparison"
    Production Day: 2 hours filming + 2 hours editing per video
    Tier 3 — 2-3x/week
    Raw & Real
    Low production, high authenticity. Goal: Connection + relatability + DMs
    Filming: Phone-shot BTS, car talks, quick tips in real time, day-in-the-life moments, no script
    Editing: Minimal—just captions, hook text, maybe a music bed
    Length: 30-90 seconds
    Platforms: Instagram Stories, TikTok, Reels, Facebook
    Examples: "Coffee talk about BAH rates", "Behind-the-scenes showing a home", "Ben's random 60-second tips"
    Production Day: 1 hour filming + 30 minutes editing per video

    Production Checklists

    Quality gates before publishing. Use these to maintain standard and catch issues before they go live.

    Tier 1: Cinematic Content Quality Gate
    Hook in first 3 seconds: Does the opening line stop the scroll? Can the viewer tell within 3 seconds what this video is about and why it matters to them?
    Visual quality: 4K resolution or best available. No shaky footage. Color grading applied. Lighting professional (no harsh shadows on faces).
    Audio quality: Dialogue clear and intelligible. No background noise. Music sits under dialogue without competing. Intro/outro music branded.
    Captions & graphics: Hard-coded captions throughout. Key statistics appear on-screen. Location/neighborhood names labeled. Font consistent (Inter).
    Story framework: Video follows 5-step structure (Hook → Problem → Journey → Resolution → CTA). Each step clear and distinct.
    Call-to-action: Final 10 seconds feature clear, one single ask. Not vague. Specific instruction (DM "PCS", visit link, etc.)
    Fact-check: All statistics verified (BAH rates, market data, school rankings). Sources current as of publish date. Disclaimers added where necessary (lending advice, legal, school info).
    Brand consistency: Logo appears on-screen. Teal accent used somewhere in design. Thumbnail follows brand template. Font choices consistent with guidelines.
    Pacing & length: No filler. Video moves purposefully. Tier 1 typically 8-15 min YouTube. Shortest edit (Shorts) is fast-paced, 60-90 seconds.
    Multiple format ready: Created main 16:9 version AND 9:16 vertical (Reels/TikTok). Different assets for different platforms? Check.
    Tier 2: Story-Wrapped Content Quality Gate
    Hook within first 3 seconds: "When you PCS for the first time..." or "You just got deployed..." Immediate relevance to viewer.
    Captions throughout: Every video must have captions (TikTok users watch muted). No exceptions.
    Clear CTA: One ask. No ambiguity. Text overlay stating the call-to-action in final 5 seconds.
    Audio intelligible: Dialogue clear. No competing background noise. Music mixed under speech at -6dB minimum.
    B-roll relevant: Supporting visuals match the narrative. If talking about neighborhoods, show neighborhoods. Don't use stock photos—show real locations.
    Length correct: Tier 2 = 2-5 minutes depending on platform. Too long for TikTok? Trim or make it Tier 1.
    Thumbnail created: Uses one of the 6 brand templates. Teal dominant. Text hierarchy clear. Readable at 200x112px.
    Facts verified: Any claims checked. If mentioning school rankings, BAH amounts, or market data, source is current and accurate.
    Tier 3: Raw & Real Quality Gate
    Authentic & unscripted: Feels genuine. No over-production. Imperfection is okay (actually preferred). Phone-quality video acceptable if content is strong.
    Captions added: Even raw content needs captions for accessibility and silent viewing. Use auto-captions if time-limited, then spot-check for accuracy.
    Hook present: Even 30-second content needs a first-line hook. "Real quick..." or "Here's a thought..." establishes relevance.
    Clear CTA: What does the viewer do next? Comment, save, share, DM, book a call? One direction, even in raw content.
    Audio acceptable: No distracting background noise. Viewers can hear you clearly. If extremely noisy environment, consider re-filming or not posting.
    Length fits platform: Stories = 15-60 sec max. TikTok = 15-60 sec. Reels = 30-90 sec. Respect platform conventions.
    Branded moment: McReynolds visible somehow. Logo in corner, your face on-screen, teal text overlay, branded intro/outro. You can't be anonymous.
    No major errors: Check spelling in overlays. Ensure captions are mostly accurate. Listen for slurred words or awkward pauses.

    Do's & Don'ts

    12 essential rules for every piece of McReynolds content. These are non-negotiable.

    DO

    Lead with a hook in every video — First 3 seconds determine if someone stays or scrolls. Make it relatable, urgent, or surprising. "When you get PCS orders..." or "Your BAH just increased..." or "You're moving your family across the country..." Not "Hi, I'm a real estate agent."
    Use "military families" and "PCS" in captions — These are SEO goldmines for your target audience. YouTube and TikTok algorithms reward these keywords. Every post mentions the military context.
    Show real homes, real neighborhoods, real people — Authenticity beats polish every time. If you show a home, it should be a home you've actually worked with. If you show a neighborhood, film on-location. Real testimonials from real clients (with permission).
    Include a clear CTA in every single post — No post without a next action. "DM us", "Book a call", "Save this", "Drop a comment", "Link in bio". Be specific and easy to act on.
    Use teal as the dominant brand color — Every video should have teal somewhere (text, logo, accent bar, thumbnail). It's your signature. Audiences recognize it in a crowded feed.
    Film in natural California light when possible — Golden hour, natural window light, outdoor settings. Avoid harsh overhead fluorescents. Light quality makes a massive difference in perceived professionalism and trustworthiness.
    Reference your track record often — "$28M+ in 2024", "150+ families", "#1 in 3 markets", "4x ICON award". Don't be shy about success. Military families want to work with winners who understand their world.
    Tag location on every post — Travis AFB, Vacaville, Fairfield, Suisun City, etc. Geotags help algorithm AND help local families find you. Location + military = discovery.
    Use military acronyms your audience knows — BAH, PCS, VA, TDY, deployment, spouse, active duty, veteran, USAF, Army, etc. Speak the language. Your audience will feel understood.
    Batch film on content day for efficiency — Dedicate one day per week (or one afternoon) to filming Tier 2 and Tier 3 content. Film 3-4 videos in one session. Same setup, different angles = more content in less time.
    Show both Sarah AND Ben — Team dynamic is a huge differentiator. Military families connect with the couple. Show you working together, complementing each other. Two veterans, both relevant, both speaking = more credible.
    Respond to DMs/comments within 2 hours — Military families are watching and responding. If someone asks "How long to close?" or "What's the BAH for O-4?", answer fast. Speed signals you care and are actually checking your account.

    DON'T

    Post without a hook or opening line — Dead air. Boring intro. Unclear relevance. These kill engagement instantly. Every post needs the first 3 seconds to work hard.
    Use stock photos of random houses — Military families know fake when they see it. Stock photos = we don't actually know the area. Show real homes, real neighborhoods. If you don't have footage, don't post about it yet.
    Give specific opinions on neighborhoods or schools — "This neighborhood is the best" or "These schools are terrible" opens you to criticism and potentially offends community members. Instead: "Here's what families love about it..." or "Data shows X, which means Y for your family."
    Give lending or legal advice — You're not a lender or attorney. "You can use your VA benefit twice" or "This is the only way to finance this" are dangerous. Always defer to professionals: "Talk to your lender about...", "Consult with a tax attorney about...", "Your loan officer can confirm..."
    Post the same content identically across all platforms — TikTok audiences see different things than YouTube audiences. Reels are different from Stories. Tailor each platform: TikTok = trending audio + fast cuts, YouTube = longer storytelling, Stories = quick moments.
    Use generic real estate hashtags without military-specific ones — #realestate #homeselling #realtor are too broad. Your hashtags should be: #TravisAFB #MilitaryFamily #PCS #VALoan #MilitaryRealEstate. Hyper-specific wins.
    Film in bad lighting or with distracting backgrounds — Poor lighting screams "unprofessional". Distracting backgrounds (clutter, other people, TV) split attention. Invest in light (even a cheap ring light). Clean background. Quality goes up, perceived authority goes up.
    Forget to add captions/subtitles to videos — 85% of social video is watched muted. No captions = no message delivery. All video, all platforms, must have captions. This is non-negotiable for engagement and accessibility.
    Go more than 2 days without posting — Consistency breeds algorithm favor. Gaps in posting = audiences move on to other accounts. Your schedule might be 1x daily across Stories + main feed, but maintain frequency. 2-day gap is a reset.
    Ignore comments or DMs — Every comment is a person raising their hand. Respond to every legitimate comment/DM. No response = you don't care = they unfollow. Response time matters more than perfection of response.
    Use overly formal/stiff language — You're veterans talking to veterans. Not corporate. Not salesy. "Let's crush this goal together" not "We facilitate real estate acquisition." "You're PCS'ing? Here's what I've learned" not "Real estate market analytics for military personnel relocation."
    Share client details without permission — Even with permission, be respectful. No last names unless approved. No family photos without clearance. No sensitive military details (exact base, deployment status, family situation) beyond what they've shared publicly.
